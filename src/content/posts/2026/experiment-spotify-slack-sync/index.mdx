**"Fredagslistan"** started because Agnes made a playlist, dropped it in Slack, and gave the first theme: **sun**.

> Here is the list! Today's theme is **sun**.

That was April 2018. A few minutes later people were joining the channel, and by the next Friday the ritual already had momentum.

Every Friday after that, someone sets a theme in the morning, people drop songs that "fit" it, and the playlist grows.

"Fit" is being generous work there. "Free interpretation" is the norm or maybe basically the rule 😅.

Here are a couple of more examples (all translated to English):

> There is a match today, so the theme is **your best match/training hype songs**! ⚽ 🥵 🤸‍♀️ Free interpretation!

> Friday! Today's theme is **flashbacks from lower secondary school**. Time for a mix of teenage personalities and decades. Let's go!

> Surely nobody missed the music-making freezers in England? Apparently fantastic music in C#. So today the theme is **instrumental music**! 🥁 🎸

For a while the playlist was maintained by hand. That worked as well as any "hand-maintained shared state" usually works: fine when someone remembered to do it.

Most people dropped tracks where the conversation already was, in Slack, and never made the second hop into Spotify. So the playlist was always a little skewed. Sometimes I inserted all the missing songs by hand, sometimes someone else did ... often nobody did. 

Then a coworker, Jonas, wrote a small python script. Which evolved to a small Python cronjob to do the transfer for us. It has been running for years now. Quietly. No heroics.

At some point it escaped work, too. At Friday dinners with friends, that we usually have every Friday, I started asking them to guess the theme from the playlist. A normal person might call that a "dinner game". I apparently decided it needed a sync job.

One thing I do love with our Friday dinners is the "bäst i veckan" (Swedish), each week we always lift one thing that was the "best of the week", I can really recommend it to anyone (and the rule is no bad things, just the good parts ... and it's not allowed to say "this dinner" either 😉, right Joel? )

## One run

### 1. Fetch from Slack

The job reads the Friday channel, extracts spotify links, and normalizes IDs. This is mostly clean up, because chat data is messy in ways APIs don't appreciate.

<ProcessStepCard step={syncSteps.fetchSlack} />

### 2. Compute the diff

Next step is simple set math: what exists in Slack but not yet in Spotify. No scoring model, no ranking, no "smart" guesswork. Just the difference.

<ProcessStepCard step={syncSteps.diff} />

### 3. Add missing songs

Missing tracks are added to the playlist. If a song is already there (within the 6 day window), the run leaves it alone. Re-runs are intentionally boring.

<ProcessStepCard step={syncSteps.addSpotify} />

There is one small design choice here that I like more than it deserves: the job only looks back a few days in Slack.

That six-day rolling window is enough to catch the current Friday and late additions without turning the whole channel history into input every time. It also means an old Spotify link someone reposts weeks later is treated like a new suggestion, which dosen't ignore songs people repost, which feels "correct". The source of truth is not "all links ever seen." It is "what people are talking about around this Fridays theme." and late additions will still be added.
