This year i made 12 new years resolutions, one for each month. Some were easy, like bicycle to work (i could scratch that because of covid-19) and others were "try taekwondo".

One of the resolutions was do Kanban with the family for one month. My first vacation week we started. We had a "family meeting", were i explained what kanban is and gave examples of how we could use it for the family.

We used magic whiteboard paper and converted a wall from "picture wall" to "kanban wall"

PIcture!!!!

After approx. 1 month we have been using the wall and having 1 week intervals (so a little more "scrumlike"). We have been having small retros every Sunday (just 10-15 min) were we have discussed what worked and what hasn't worked. I've also run stop, start and continue, a classic retro exercise.

Every morning after breakfast we have had a short gathering in front of the board and assigned "tickets" for the day.

### Reflection

The "effort, reward" is very visual through kanban and works extra well for children. They can see what should be done when we start, they also get to adress if they have too much, like I don't want to carry out the trash three times this week and we can discuss together what a reasonable amount should be or if we want to exchange chores

They can also see all their hard work in the done column at the end of the week. Which gives them a sense of accomplishment.

### What now?

We have been testing this during the summer, where the weeks sometimes has involved traveling and visiting friends and other weeks there has been midsummer or other things we've had to work around. At one point we re furnished, painted and fixed up the whole hallway (where we had the kanban board 😱 ) ... so for a time we had to take the board down.

I hope and would like to continue this experiment during the fall, but one thing that we had and have issues with is the size of a task. Some tasks is "carry out the trash" and others are do the laundry, one task takes 10 minutes (if you're slow) and the other takes a minimum of 1,5 hours (not constantly, but you need to stay at home and make room/time for it). It would be nice to find a better balance without having breakdowns and the resulting 300 post it's on the board.

And maybe look into another assignment variation than the current swimlanes (or maybe they work, i'm not sure 🤔)
