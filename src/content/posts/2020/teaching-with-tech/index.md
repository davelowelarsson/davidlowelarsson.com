As parents we, me and my wife, usually take a couple of hours each week to help our kids with home work or teach our kids in general. Some days its just "how to get fortnite working", other days it's help with reading or math and somedays we build a arduino project together.

This article is a brain to keyboard thoughts about how we use tech when we help our kids.

\## English

Usually when I read with my children. The books are in Swedish but I translate each sentence to English after reading it in Swedish (or sometimes vice versa). This helps them grasp English in general a bit better.

My oldest is learning her mother tongue, English, as an extra class. This usually means we read or write english as well as Swedish.

Recently we have been working on spelling and grammar and for that she usually wrote her text and then I read it, then I gave her pointers on how to improve.

Then she wrote a word that I also was unsure how to spell, so I checked Google translate. Which, i realised, she also could use on her own phone. So I showed her the app and then she could use swedish to find out the english words. Usually a swedish word can mean several different english words ... so its not perfect, but a good tool anyhow to learn and explore words.
